Lost Kids, founded by Brent Faiyaz and Ty Baisden, is an independent collective blending music, fashion, film, and entrepreneurship. With an alternative, emotion-driven aesthetic, it prioritizes creative freedom and authenticity, attracting artists who share the same vision.

About
us

Founded by American singer Brent Faiyaz and his manager Ty Baisden, Lost Kids is far more than just a music label. It’s a true artistic and entrepreneurial collective. The name comes from a group of Brent’s high school friends: “we were just lost kids.” This phrase perfectly captures the mindset behind the project, young people living outside the norms, finding their path through the streets, fashion, and creativity. Always “fly,” always different.

Musically, Lost Kids is a unique blend of alternative R&B, soul, rap/trap, and minimalist, atmospheric production, often inspired by the sounds of the 1990s and early 2000s. The label stands out for its rejection of conventions and its sincere approach: for Brent, more than the kicks or snares, what truly matters is the feeling and the message. This philosophy runs through every project, where music becomes a form of raw, emotional expression.

But Lost Kids goes far beyond music. The label also extends into fashion, start-ups, film, start-ups, real estate investments, and philanthropic initiatives, reflecting a broad vision of creativity and independence. Brent and Ty have always stated that their goal was to keep full control over their art and their ventures, without depending on major record labels. Lost Kids therefore embodies artistic and financial autonomy, as well as a culture of experimentation and freedom.

The spirit of the label is deeply community-driven. It remains true to its origin as a crew, a group of lost but passionate young people, fighting to find meaning in a confusing world. This marginal and alternative energy has become both an aesthetic and ideological signature, attracting artists who share the same pursuit of authenticity and depth.
